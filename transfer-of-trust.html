Not immediately and not fully. Jordan is staying more involved in the near term — focused on innovation: AI systems, client-getting strategies, and content that gets fed directly to you. Why Taeler? How did you choose her?
Answer Editable
We interviewed multiple buyers and operators — people inside and outside the industry, with very different visions. We chose Taeler for four reasons: (1) she's committed to protecting our mission and legacy; (2) she genuinely knows and cares about her clients at a deep level; (3) she is relentlessly competitive — which means she'll stay innovative and always bring you the best strategies; and (4) she's in the season of life to go all the way in. Erin is fully in on motherhood right now. IFCA deserves someone fully in on the business. Taeler is that person.

Who is Taeler De Haes?
Answer Editable
Taeler is the founder of Fitness Coach Academy (FCA). She started with $825 in her bank account after being laid off from a TV news career, grew her own fitness business to $250K in year one using primarily organic strategies, and built FCA into one of the top fitness business coaching programs in the space over five years — growing to 650+ coaches. She has been running parallel to IFCA for years. She's not a corporate buyer. She lived this industry from the ground up, just like we did.

What does this mean for me practically — what's actually changing?
Answer Editable
In the near term: very little changes in your day-to-day experience. Your success coach stays, your curriculum stays, your coaching calls stay. What changes over time is that IFCA gains new resources, new strategies (particularly around organic growth and DM-setting), and a CEO who is all-the-way-in on taking this company to the next level. You're getting more, not less.
Q07
What happens to FCA students and the FCA program?

FCA will be integrated into IFCA over the coming months. FCA clients will have access to IFCA's curriculum, team, and resources as that transition happens. All FCA contracts are honored exactly as written during the process.

Will the IFCA curriculum change?
Answer Editable
The foundational curriculum and frameworks that make IFCA one of the most respected programs in this space are staying. Taeler is committed to protecting what works. What you'll see over time is additions and upgrades — not gutting what's been built. Taeler brings specific expertise in organic growth and DM-setting strategy that will be folded in.

Yes, through the transition period (Q3 2026). They're both reachable through your success coach or via the student community. For urgent matters, reply to the student email — it routes to the right person.
